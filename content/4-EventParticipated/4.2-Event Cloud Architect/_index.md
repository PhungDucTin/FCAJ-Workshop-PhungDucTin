# Post-Event Report "Cloud Architect"

<h4 align="center"><em></em></h4>

![Poster](/images/4-EventParticipated/4.2-Event Cloud Architect/Evt_2.png)

### Event Objectives

- Create a simulated playground for real-world decision-making and system architecture design in the AWS cloud environment.
- Comprehensively test and reinforce knowledge ranging from foundational levels (Cloud Practitioner) to solution design capabilities (Solutions Architect Associate - SAA) and high-level architectural thinking (Solutions Architect Professional - SAP).
- Practice time allocation, risk management, and problem-solving tactics under high pressure through a tiered scoring system.
- Connect the IT student community and Cloud enthusiasts, setting the stage for applying theoretical knowledge from the classroom to real-world infrastructure problems.

---

### Key Highlights: Escalating Challenge Structure

The competition was designed with escalating difficulty and point weighting, requiring participants to constantly adapt their strategies.

#### Qualifying Round: Building the Foundation (10 questions)
- **Structure:** 7 Cloud Practitioner (CP) questions, 2 SAA questions, 1 SAP question.
- **Focus:** This round primarily focused on mastering basic AWS concepts, pricing models, and core services. The inclusion of 1 SAP question (worth 50 points) right from the start acted as a distractor, testing risk assessment skills: whether to invest time to grab 50 points or quickly complete the 7 foundational questions (70 points) to preserve energy.

#### Semi-Final Round: Mindset Shift (10 questions)
- **Structure:** 5 CP questions, 3 SAA questions, 2 SAP questions.
- **Focus:** The number of foundational questions decreased, making way for architectural design problems. This stage required an understanding of how services interact, particularly in establishing virtual private clouds (VPC), system security, and access control. The two SAP questions played a role in clearly differentiating scores among contestants.

#### Final Round: Expert-Level Challenge (10 questions)
- **Structure:** 3 CP questions, 4 SAA questions, 3 SAP questions.
- **Focus:** This was the decisive round, where the total points from SAP questions (150 points) accounted for a massive proportion. The problems went beyond whether a system could simply run; they had to meet strict standards for high availability, cost optimization, and, especially, deep cybersecurity in large-scale enterprise environments.

#### Power-ups (Support Skills)
Each team was provided with 2 tactical skills to alter the score dynamics:
1. **Star of Hope (High Risk - High Reward):** A correct answer earns double points, but an incorrect one deducts double the question's points.
2. **Risk Mitigation (Safe Play):** A correct answer earns only half (50%) of the points, but an incorrect one results in no point deduction.

---

### Key Takeaways

#### Professional & Technical Knowledge
- **Cloud Architecture Tiering:** The distinction between the 3 levels (CP, SAA, SAP) helped me realize the gap between understanding how a service operates (e.g., running a Linux server) and designing an entire system capable of auto-scaling and multi-region fault tolerance.
- **Security-Centric Mindset:** The toughest questions at the SAP level typically revolved around sensitive data management, encryption, and establishing multi-layered security guardrails. This strongly reinforced a cybersecurity mindset, from detailed IAM configuration to operating automated data protection tools.

#### Soft Skills & Tactics
- **Score Optimization Strategy:** Allocating time logically based on question value. Sometimes, skipping a stuck CP question (10 points) to focus on deducing an SAP question (50 points) provided a significant advantage.
- **Decision-Making Under Pressure:** Learned how to quickly scan keywords in lengthy SAA and SAP prompts to eliminate trap options, keeping a cool head when facing questions beyond current knowledge limits.

---

### Event Experience

Competing in the "Cloud Architect" event was a tense but incredibly valuable practical training experience, helping me transform lines of code or isolated configuration actions into a holistic picture of infrastructure architecture.

#### Real-World Pressure
- Facing the surging difficulty in the Final Round was truly a test of mettle. Having to solve 3 Professional-level questions simultaneously required intense concentration and the ability to analyze complex logical chains.

#### Exposure and Self-Assessment
- The competition served as a perfect benchmark to self-"audit" my capabilities. The tough questions helped me identify gaps in my design knowledge, thereby providing a clearer direction for my upcoming advanced training roadmap.

#### Lessons Learned
- Scores in architecture exams reflect not just memory, but Problem-Solving capabilities.
- Foundational knowledge (Practitioner) is a necessary condition to avoid losing easy points, but high-level solution thinking (SAP) and security acumen are the sufficient conditions to break through.
- This experience provided massive motivation to continue diving deep into practical AWS labs, turning textbook theory into natural reflexes when designing systems.
