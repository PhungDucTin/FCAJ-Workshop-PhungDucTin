### Purpose of the Event

- To create a playground that practically simulates the decision-making and system architecture design process in the AWS cloud computing environment.
- To comprehensively test and consolidate knowledge from the foundational level (Cloud Practitioner) to solution design capabilities (Solutions Architect Associate - SAA) and high-level architectural mindset (Solutions Architect Professional - SAP).
- To practice time allocation, risk management, and problem-solving tactics under high pressure through a tiered scoring system.
- To connect the community of information technology students and Cloud enthusiasts, laying the groundwork for applying theoretical knowledge from the classroom to practical infrastructure problems.

---

### Highlights: Escalating Challenge Structure

The competition was designed with progressive difficulty and point weighting, requiring participants to continuously change their tactics.

#### Qualifying Round: Building the Foundation (10 questions)
- **Structure:** 7 Cloud Practitioner (CP) questions, 2 SAA questions, 1 SAP question.
- **Focus:** This round primarily focused on mastering basic AWS concepts, pricing models, and core services. The appearance of 1 SAP question (worth 50 points) right from the start acted as a distractor, challenging risk assessment capabilities: whether to invest time to get 50 points or quickly finish the 7 foundational questions (70 points) to preserve energy.

#### Semifinals: Paradigm Shift (10 questions)
- **Structure:** 5 CP questions, 3 SAA questions, 2 SAP questions.
- **Focus:** The number of foundational questions decreased, giving way to architectural design problems. This stage required an understanding of how services interact with each other, especially setting up virtual network layers (VPC), system security, and access authorization. The two SAP questions played a role in clearly differentiating scores among candidates.

#### Final Round: Expert Level Challenge (10 questions)
- **Structure:** 3 CP questions, 4 SAA questions, 3 SAP questions.
- **Focus:** This was the decisive round where the total points from the SAP questions (150 points) accounted for a massive proportion. The problems did not just stop at whether the system could run or not, but had to meet strict standards for high availability, cost optimization, and especially in-depth cybersecurity in a large-scale enterprise environment.

#### Power-ups (Support Skills)
Each team was provided with 2 tactical skills to change the scoring situation:
1. **Star of Hope (High Risk - High Reward):** A correct answer doubles the points added, but an incorrect answer doubles the points deducted for that question.
2. **Risk Mitigation (Safe Play):** A correct answer only adds half (50%) the points, but an incorrect answer results in no point deduction.

### What I Learned

#### Professional & Technical Knowledge
- **Cloud Architecture Stratification:** The difference between the 3 levels (CP, SAA, SAP) helped me realize the gap between understanding how a service operates (e.g., running a Linux server) and designing an entire system capable of auto-scaling and multi-region fault tolerance.
- **Security-Centric Mindset:** The most challenging questions at the SAP level often revolved around managing sensitive data, encryption, and setting up multi-layered security barriers. This strongly reinforced my cybersecurity mindset, from detailed IAM configuration to operating automated data protection tools.

#### Soft Skills & Tactics
- **Score Optimization Strategy:** Allocating time reasonably based on the value of the question. Sometimes, skipping a CP question (10 points) that you are stuck on to focus on deducing an SAP question (50 points) brings an overwhelming advantage.
- **Decision Making Under Pressure:** Learning to quickly read keywords in lengthy SAA and SAP prompts to eliminate trap options, keeping a cool head when facing questions beyond current knowledge limits.

---

### Experience during the event

Competing in the "Cloud Architect" event was a highly stressful but extremely valuable practical training experience, helping me transform lines of code or single configuration actions into an overall picture of infrastructure architecture.

#### Practical Pressure
- Facing the soaring difficulty in the Final Round was truly a test of mettle. Having to solve up to 3 Professional-level questions simultaneously required intense concentration and the ability to analyze complex logical chains.

#### Engagement and Self-Assessment
- The competition was a perfect benchmark to self-"audit" my abilities. The tricky questions helped me realize gaps in my design knowledge, thereby giving clearer direction for my upcoming in-depth training roadmap.

#### Lessons Learned
- Scores in architectural exams do not just reflect memory, but the ability to handle problems (Problem Solving).
- Foundational knowledge (Practitioner) is a necessary condition to avoid losing easy points, but a high-level solution mindset (SAP) and security acumen are the sufficient conditions to break through.
- This experience created huge motivation to continue delving into hands-on AWS labs, turning textbook theory into natural reflexes when designing systems.
